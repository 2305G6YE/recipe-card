1: Beef Stew

Description

I stumbled upon this dish when I was looking for something hearty I could put together in winter. When it is that time of the year generally people would want something nourishing and warm. I altered the base recipe myself a little bit more to give a kick using alternate meat sources around my area, and so can you! Just bear in mind some meats do not do as well as others when it comes to long simmering, so I recommend beef.

Items

(Serving Size: 8)

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 750g beef chunk cubes
  • 1 medium yellow onion(diced)
  • 2 carrots(peeled and chopped into rounds)
  • 2 stalks of celery(chopped)
  • 4 clove of garlic(finely chopped)
  • 31g tomato paste
  • 1L(to liking) chicken stock
  • 3pkts Worcestershire sauce
  • 500g potatoes(peeled and cut into halves)
  • 250g frozen peas
  • salt and pepper(to liking)

2: Mapo Tofu

Description

I think the most difficult part of this dish is nailing down that tangy mouth-piercing spice. But so long as you have enough chilli variations, you should not need those specialized spices you read on other websites! This is a recipe I somewhat improved upon from the net after tasting it myself a few times, so I hope you like it!

Items

(Serving Size: 2)

  • 90ml olive oil
  • 2 fresh chilli peppers (roughly chopped)
  • 8 dried red chillis (roughly chopped)
  • 21/2tbsp ginger (finely minced)
  • 21/2tbsp garlic (finely minced)
  • 200ml chicken stock
  • 450g silken tofu (cut into size of choice)
  • 250g minced pork
  • 3 tbsp spicy bean sauce
  • 11/2tbsp cornstarch
  • 50ml water
